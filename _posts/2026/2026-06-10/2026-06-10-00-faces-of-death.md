<span class="reviewsection">Premise:</span> Barbie Ferreira works as a content moderator in this not-tiktok app thing, and as such she gets to see all kinds of dumb and messed up stuff for a living. On the other hand though at least it is not Youtube so she doesn't need to watch (*shivers*) four hour long video essays, so I guess it's not all bad. Anyway! One day she starts to run into these extremely-late-70s-looking videos that have a creepy narration and show mock deaths on screen... or are they!? What if they were real even though they look crappy as hell?<br/>

<span class="reviewsection">Under 90 minutes?</span> Very very close but no.<br/>
<span class="reviewsection">Do they say the title?</span> Yeah. I mean, the original ***Faces of Death*** is a plot point so of course they keep saying the name, which is definitely a good thing if you ask me.

<span class="reviewsection">First thing I thought of:</span>

![faces-of-death](faces-of-death.gif)<br/>
<span class="reviewsection">Okay, more:</span> I was an *Eighties Kid™* and very into horror stuff (that terrified me back then), so of course I was more than aware of the existence of ***Faces of Death***. Some kid in another class swore that he knew someone who had DIED because they watched the movie, which in hindsight is laughable because that's what happens when you watch ***Ringu***, but I digress.<br/>So... this is a really campy, cheesy movie that (let's be very honest here) often feels like an ***American Horror Story*** episode. And yet, it is also fun and it feels funny at times to see Kids These Days with Their Tiktoks talking about some basically-urban-legend movie from decades ago. There's this very inspired scene where the killer posts a video and right after that it immediately gets a million comments and people talking about it "in real life", which feels 100% like "if we had had the internet then, this would have played out exactly like that". There's even a satanic panic kind of news report! So yeah, this gets a thumbs up.<br/>

<span class="reviewsection">A simple way to improve it:</span> honestly, I'm missing more of that wonderful montage like the one by the end of [***hashtagchadgetstheaxe***]({% post_url 2025/2025-10-21/2025-10-21-00-chadgetstheaxe %}), specially people doing silly dances while pretending to mimic the kills, etc. There's unfortunately only like twenty seconds of that tops and it would have been so good.

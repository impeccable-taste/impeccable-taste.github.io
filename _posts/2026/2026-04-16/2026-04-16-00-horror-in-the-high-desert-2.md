<span class="reviewsection">Premise:</span> A loosely (to put it midly) connected sequel to [***Horror in the High Desert***]({% post_url 2025/2025-04-15/2025-04-15-00-horror-in-the-high-desert %}), this is the story of the titular Minerva going to the High Desert to find Horror, I guess. Anyway so she goes to Midonowhere Town, then they find her but dead in bad circumstances, honestly. And there's also another lady whose name I can't remember that is a mom and honestly this is confusing BUT there's some footage that was found and put together as a fake documentary so here we are.<br/>

<span class="reviewsection">Under 90 minutes?</span> Yeah! Blissfully short, about 75 minutes long.<br/>
<span class="reviewsection">Do they say the title?</span> Nah.

<span class="reviewsection">Okay, more:</span> The first movie had an afterthought of a plot, this one is basically a series of spooky scenes interconnected by some attempt at a narrative. At no point we are going to get any kind of answers, the documentary format barely makes any sense considering there's nothing happening in terms of an investigation other than a couple of interviews... And at the same time boy were those some *really* tense scenes, specially that one with the dude walking in pitch black darkness (but you, the viewer, can still see stuff thanks to the camera's night vision). I think in this case the runtime *really* works because if it was a bit longer than this i would have been annoyed, but at "barely a bit over an hour" it's kind of an okay watch if you like the genre, I guess? Whoa another thoughtful, informative review by the Impeccable-Taste HQ!!!<br/>

<span class="reviewsection">A simple way to improve it:</span> Number stations, old timey recordings and drone music are by definition really unsettling so I say more of that, all the time, and even more of the (tasteful) "did i see something in the background of that shot?" thing.

<span class="reviewsection">Premise:</span> So picture this: you're walking through the woods, there's no one around and your phone is dead. [Out of the corner of your eye, y-](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o0u4M6vppCI) oh... Oh I see. Okay so this is a movie where a woman who was looking for a cryptid and found amnesia is now looking for her own identity. Sort of. What she finds will surprise you!<br/>

<span class="reviewsection">Under 90 minutes?</span> Nah, fifteen minutes over that.<br/>
<span class="reviewsection">Do they say the title?</span> Not exactly, I think.

<span class="reviewsection">First thing I thought of:</span>

![ewww hahaha](kryptic.gif)<br/>
<span class="reviewsection">Okay, more:</span> Yeah, so this is an oozefest of a psychological/body horror movie (laudatory). It is, very very faithful to its title, pretty cryptic at times, it really does not want to give you way too easy a ride and it feels like one of those anxiety dreams where things *almost* make sense, but they don't, really, and it is pretty oppressive because of exactly that.<br/>Or, if you prefer, it's like your favourite song, but the otamatone cover. It's almost there, but it is also sorta off in a way that you can't really describe.<br/> Anyhoo, I liked it a lot, was very intrigued, the husband character *seriously* creeped me out. Maybe at times would have preferred a *slightly* easier to follow narrative, but, you know. Along for the ride, babeh.

<span class="reviewsection">A simple way to improve it:</span> I like that this movie knows it can get very gross and have jizz all over the place all the time, just like on the previously reviewed [***Girl on the Third Floor***]({% post_url 2025/2025-05-10/2025-05-10-00-girl-on-the-third-floor %}). I feel that we should get over the taboo of having really disgusting body fluids on screen and instead we should, just like this film does, *comment* on how it smells (bad) and how it feels (sticky. okay, well, and more stuff). Yes, I consider myself an intellectual.
